VERBAL VIOLENCE

Verbal violence is a form of aggression that, although it does not leave visible marks, can cause deep wounds in a person's self-esteem and emotional well-being. Unlike physical violence, verbal violence is often normalized and minimized in many environments, such as school, work, and family, making it even more dangerous.

In my opinion, I am against verbal violence because it destroys trust and respect between people. No one should feel belittled or intimidated by others through words.

Language has great power, and it is our responsibility to use it positively, promoting dialogue and empathy instead of harm and aggression. Respect and assertive communication are key to building healthy and harmonious relationships.
